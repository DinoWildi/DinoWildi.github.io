My name is Dino Wildi. I am a political scientist focusing on party strategy, new parties, and wedge issues in Europe.

Currently I am working in the ClimateDivide project headed by [Endre Borbáth](https://endre-borbath.eu/) at the University of Heidelberg, Germany as a postdoctoral researcher. The project focuses on researching the increasing politicisation of climate change policies, and the actors that drive this process in a set of seven European countries. We are working on extracting political claims and positions on climate change from newspaper articles using advanced text analysis methods.

In previous projects, I have worked on party strategies in response to the rise of the radical right in Europe, focusing on comparative quantitative methods. I have published in Party Politics, and have forthcoming work on negative campaigning targeting the radical right, and on the possibility of challenging the radical right from a left-conservative position in Germany.

Outside my research interests, I enjoy music and playing the guitar, binding books, and playing board and card games.
